A Little Bit About Me

I'm an aspiring software developer with a passion for web development and UI/UX design. I graduated from the one true university of Texas, Texas A&M University, with a degree in industrial engineering. Now I know what you're probably thinking, what does industrial engineering have to do with software development? Well... absolutely nothing. By the time I realized what I really wanted to do in life, I was already 3.5 years and $30,000 in, so there was no turning back. However, I am always looking for ways to leverage my engineering knowledge and creativity to develop applications that solve real-world problems.

When that carpal tunnel sets in after 12 hours of coding, I rejuvenate my soul by hiking, driving down country roads, and blasting Taylor Swift.

What I Do

Define

The most critical step in any project is defining the problem, user requirements, limitations, timetable, and the project deliverables. Expect me to ask questions such as:
1. What is the problem?
2. Who has the problem?
3. Why is it important to solve?

Design

Most people only associate the design of an application with its' aesthetics. However, I believe the functionality and user experience are just as important to consider when in the design stage. Before I ever write a line of code, I like to put my vision on paper and bounce ideas off the client to ensure they're included in the process, and layout exactly how I'm going to solve their problem.

Develop

This is where computers are thrown across the room, tears are shed, and my patience is pushed to its limit. But, this is where dreams are turned into reality. The beautiful thing about being a developer is you're constantly having to overcome some new problem, but get a rush of euphoria when you inevitably breakthrough them. I honestly have an addiction to that feeling, and it's what keeps me coming back for more projects.
